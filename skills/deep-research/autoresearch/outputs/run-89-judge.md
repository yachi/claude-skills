### Fact-Check Details
- Claim 1: "TMSR-LF1 is a 2MWt prototype that achieved thorium-uranium conversion in November 2025" — Verified: **YES** — World Nuclear News, CAS, and Power Magazine all confirm the 2MWt capacity, October 2024 thorium loading, and November 2025 announcement of successful Th-U conversion ([WNN](https://www.world-nuclear-news.org/articles/chinese-msr-achieves-conversion-of-thorium-uranium-fuel)).
- Claim 2: "IAEA Milestones Approach: 10-15 years from initial consideration to first NPP operation" — Verified: **YES** — IAEA NG-G-3.1 Rev. 2 explicitly states this timeline reflecting Member State experience ([IAEA](https://www.iaea.org/topics/infrastructure-development/milestones-approach)).
- Claim 3: "MIT projects AP1000 NOAK overnight cost at $4,625/kW with LCOE of $66/MWh" — Verified: **YES** — MIT CANES 2024 report confirms these figures, with $50/MWh achievable with 40% ITC ([MIT](https://canes.mit.edu/media/overnight-capital-cost-of-the-next-ap1000)).

### Missing Angles
- **India's thorium program history**: India has pursued thorium for 50+ years with limited success — this historical precedent would further strengthen the case against MSR.
- **Financing availability**: Vendor-country export credit agencies (US EXIM, KEXIM, Rosatom financing) are critical for new nuclear countries; this angle is briefly mentioned but could be expanded.
